Meal of the Month

Tilapia and Cassava over white Rice

Video courtesy of Cooking the Cassava Leaves.

Tilapia

  • 6-8 pieces of Tilapia Fish
  • 1/2 cup of Adobe seasoning
  • 2 tbsp of vegetable oil
  • 2 tbsp of salt

Preheat the oven to 375. Add a tbsp of vegetable oil per piece of Fish and a tsp of salt. Place on a cookie sheet and place in the oven. Cook for 40 mins, then let sit with oven turned off for 5 mins.

Rice

  • 3 cups of Rice
  • 3 cups of boiling water
  • 1 tsp of salt
  • 1 tbls of vegetable oil

Bring 6 cups of water to a boil. Add a tbsp of vegetable oil and a tsp of salt. Add 3 cups of rice. Cook on high for 1o mins, then simmer on low for 5 mins.

Cassava

  • 2 packs of frozen Ground Cassava Leaves
  • 6 cups of water
  • 1/2 cup of palm oil
  • 2 cups of dice bell peppers
  • 1 cup of diced onions

Bring 6 cups of water to a boil. Add 2 packs of frozen Ground Cassava Leaves. Add 1/2 cup of palm oil. Add garlic and a tbsp of salt (and other desired spices to taste). Add 2 cups of dice bell peppers and 1 cup of diced onions. Cook on high for 40 mins, then simmer on low for 20 mins.

Home

This page welcomes the user to our site in a warm way. It takes them to a very familiar place, their kitchen! The reason anyone would use my site over using another persons website is because of the end user experience. The user wants something that is user friendly and that makes it easy for them to navigate. I intend to make this like a locked scroll for the logo bar on the home page which will include banners, logos, and navigation bars/links. I will also note the title of each page so that the user can refer to it to be reminded of what he is currently looking at. I think it will be important to refrain from busy work in my coding as these effects the experience of the user as well. They may be amused at first by the graphics and such, but I recognize that it could be a distraction and take away from what they are trying to do.
